A Gratuitous Permit (GP) is a special authorization issued by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) that allows an individual or institution to collect, study, or transport wildlife and/or its derivatives for scientific, educational, or conservation purposesâ€”without any commercial intent. It is issued alongside a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA), which outlines the roles, responsibilities, and conditions governing the activity.

The GP and MOA are essential for ensuring that any use of wildlife resources for non-commercial purposes is aligned with the policies of the Wildlife Resources Conservation and Protection Act (RA 9147). These permits help regulate access and ensure conservation efforts are upheld.
