A Research Clearance (Marine) is an official permit issued by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) through its regional offices, allowing individuals or institutions to conduct scientific research and studies in coastal and marine areas within protected zones. This clearance ensures that all research activities align with conservation objectives and national environmental policies, while also monitoring the impact of such activities on marine biodiversity.

ðŸ“‹ Example Requests

  • Marine biodiversity assessment in coral reef ecosystems within a Protected Area
  • Water quality monitoring and sampling in marine reserves
  • Tagging and tracking of marine species (e.g., sea turtles, reef fish) for population studies
