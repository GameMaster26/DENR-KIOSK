üìò What are Wildlife Farm Permits (WFP)?

A Wildlife Farm Permit (WFP) is a legal authorization issued by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) allowing individuals or entities to engage in the breeding, rearing, or raising of wildlife species in captivity for conservation, trade, or scientific purposes. The permit ensures that wildlife farming is done in a manner that supports biodiversity, adheres to ethical standards, and complies with environmental laws such as R.A. No. 9147 or the Wildlife Resources Conservation and Protection Act.

üìã Example Requests

  • Application for breeding of exotic birds for conservation and trade
  • Establishment of a private wildlife rescue and rehabilitation facility
  • Captive breeding and farming of reptiles or amphibians for scientific research
