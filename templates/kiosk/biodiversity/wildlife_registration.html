üìò What are Certificate of Wildlife Registration?

A Certificate of Wildlife Registration (CWR) is an official document issued by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) to individuals or entities in possession of wildlife species, whether acquired legally through breeding, rescue, or importation.

This certificate ensures that wildlife in captivity is accounted for, monitored, and maintained in accordance with Republic Act No. 9147, also known as the Wildlife Resources Conservation and Protection Act.

üìã Example

  • Private individual registering wildlife species such as exotic birds or reptiles for legal possession.
  • Wildlife rescue centers documenting rescued and rehabilitated animals in their care.
  • Research institutions keeping wildlife for scientific and educational purposes.
