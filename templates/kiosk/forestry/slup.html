ðŸ“˜ What is a Tree Cutting/Earth Balling Permit for Government Projects Special Land Use Permit (SLUP) Application

A Tree Cutting/Earth Balling Permit for Government Projects under a Special Land Use Permit (SLUP) is a formal authorization issued by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) allowing the temporary use of forestland for government infrastructure projects. This includes activities such as road construction, flood control, power transmission, or other public utilities where trees within the project area may be affected. The permit ensures that environmental safeguards are met, appropriate compensation or mitigation is implemented, and legal requirements are followed.

ðŸ“„ Purpose of the Special Land Use Permit (SLUP) Application

  • To regulate and formalize the temporary use of forestland areas for government infrastructure and development projects.
  • To ensure that tree cutting and/or earth balling is conducted legally, with proper assessment and mitigation measures in place.
  • To safeguard the ecological integrity of the affected area while balancing the development needs of government projects.
