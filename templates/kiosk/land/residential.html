üìò What is Residential Free Patent?

The Residential Free Patent Application (RFPA) is a legal process under Republic Act No. 10023 that allows qualified Filipinos to apply for land titles over residential lands they have been occupying. This program simplifies land titling by authorizing the DENR to issue free patents over untitled, alienable and disposable residential lands.

üìã Requirements

  • Duly accomplished application form for Residential Free Patent
  • Proof of actual occupation and possession of the land (e.g., tax declarations, affidavits, barangay certification)
  • Sketch plan or lot data computation prepared by a licensed geodetic engineer
  • Proof of Filipino citizenship (e.g., birth certificate, valid ID)
  • Certification from CENRO that the land is alienable and disposable

Notes:

  • One (1) application per applicant for Residential Free Patent.
  • The maximum area for the application of Residential Free Patent is:
    • 200 sq. m. for highly urbanized cities
    • 500 sq. m. for other cities
    • 750 sq. m. for first to second class municipalities
    • 1,000 sq. m. for third class and below / all others
  • Per RA No. 10023, the CENR Office is allowed a maximum of 120 calendar days to process the RFPA application, while the PENR Officer is allowed a maximum of 5 working days.
